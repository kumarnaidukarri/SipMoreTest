About Tandoori Tea:

  • Tandoori tea is traditionally served in kullad cups, which are small, handcrafted clay cups used in Indian tea culture.
  • These kullad cups are highly valued for their ability to retain heat and impart a unique, smoky flavor to the tea.
  • Unlike plastic cups, kullads are eco-friendly and reusable, making them a sustainable choice for enjoying tea.
  • They are crafted from natural clay and are designed to be used multiple times, contributing to a reduced environmental footprint.
  • The use of kullad cups enhances the traditional tea-drinking experience, combining the rich taste of the tea with the rustic charm of clay.
  • By avoiding plastic, kullads support a more environmentally conscious lifestyle while celebrating cultural heritage.

Benefits and Eco-Friendliness of Tandoori Tea:

  • Unique Flavor: Tandoori tea is renowned for its distinctive smoky flavor, imparted by traditional preparation. Drinking tea from a clay cup can have some health benefits. Clay is known to naturally regulate temperature, keeping the tea warm for a longer period.
  • Heat Retention: The use of clay cups, or kullads, enhances heat retention due to clay's insulating properties. This is because clay retains the heat inside the cup, keeping the tea at an optimal drinking temperature.
  • Environmentally Friendly: Tandoori tea is served in eco-friendly kullad cups made from natural clay. These are biodegradable.
  • Health Benefits: Clay as a Natural Element: Drinking from clay cups is believed to have health benefits. Clay is a natural material that can balance the acidity of beverages and provide some minerals.

About Tandoori Masala Tea

  • Unique Flavor: The smoky, rich flavor of the tea, infused with spices, offers a delightful taste experience.

Eco-Friendly Benefits of Tandoori Masala Chai:

  1. Clay Cups (Kulhads):

    Traditionally, tandoori chai is served in eco-friendly, disposable earthen cups known as kulhads. These clay cups are biodegradable and reduce plastic waste.

  2. Natural Ingredients:

    Tandoori masala chai is made from natural ingredients like spices (cardamom, ginger, cinnamon, cloves), tea leaves, and milk.

  3. Local and Traditional Methods:

    Tandoori chai is often prepared using traditional techniques, like using a tandoor to heat the clay cups, minimizing the need for energy-consuming machinery.

  4. Reduction of Single-Use Plastics:

    By using kulhads, Tandoori Masala Chai vendors avoid single-use plastic cups, which contribute to plastic pollution.
